The Problem of Paper Medical Records

Although free and open source medical record software exists, the vast majority of clinics and hospitals in developing healthcare setting still rely on paper medical records. A host of barriers, from training for staff adoption to a simple lack of access to required technology, ensure that a wealth of valuable data remains trapped on paper, limiting our ability to do everything from diseae surveillance and resource allocation to monitoring outcomes to improve quality of care.

Mae Tao Clinic's Story

At the Mae Tao Clinic this issue of data entrapment readily apparent. Operating for 30 years in the middle of the world's longest-running civil war, the Mae Tao Clinic offers essential health care services to migrants and refugees along the Thai-Burma Border. Its Health Information System has proved an essential tool during that time, allowing them to do disease surveillence and monitor quality of care. However in its current state it is limited by the need for manual data entry.

Our Approach for Medical Record Digitization

Using software-based OMR to digitize medical information, targeting data capture in developing healthcare settings stuck in the gap between needing healthcare informatics and having the resources necessary to support a full EMR system. At Mae Tao Clinic, this gives us the rare opportunity to use patient-level data in a developing healthcare setting to better understand maternal health outcomes in this unique setting and improve quality of care for the vulnerable patient population. Its forte is its reproductive health department, which has supported 1500+ births per year for more than a decade now.

Our Vision

Our goal is to create a general-purpose solution for sustainable, robust data collection in developing healthcare settings.
Getting data off the page and enabling everything from predictive healthcare analytics to resource optimization and
data-driven research into the drivers of healthcare outcomes in regions that need it most.

Timeline

1) December 2019: Deploy in MTC's RH Department
2) Summer 2020: Expand to other departments at MTC
3) 2020 and Beyond: Package for deployment anywhere

Enabling Data-Driven Research in a Unique Setting

Initial analysis of MTC's HIS revealed interesting correlations between ANC care and delivery outcomes.
With more robust patient-level information, we could do more specific trend analysis to better understand
the specific obstetric risks driving outcomes in MTC's patient population.

Bringing the Benefits of AI to Those in Need

Modern analytic approaches, from computer vision to statistical learning, are often restricted
to high-resource settings. This project is a new effort in using AI to help those in need.
The technology is there, and the need for better health informatics is clear. It's up to us to put it all together.
